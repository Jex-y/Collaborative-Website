Why I think Technology is Great
Technology is everywhere and in every single element of human life in the modern world
In this short series of pages I hope to showcase the significant positive impacts offered by technology and computing.


I believe the most significant positive changes are in:



Almost everyday you hear news of some medical breakthrough, new technologies such as an ALS voice synthesizer, which allows patients suffering from ALS to have their voice reheard by remaking it from past audio clips, futuristic looking bionic limbs, or even the more day to day uses of administrative book keeping automated by computers. Such as accesiblity of organs for transplant or making sure the regions with more asthmatic people are stocked with more inhalers. All of these are enabled by technology and (or) computing.

Automation, we have automated many aspects of businesses. Accounting to manufacturing to administrative tasks, this has changed the way we run business nowadays, for the better. One benefit is that you can start a business from your house, register and do all the relevant and legal paperwork without leaving your house. This level of automation also means that certain dangerous jobs are being handled by robots. All around technology and computing have optimised business which intern has sped up the economy which is a good thing for nearly everyone.

Machines do not need to sleep and because of that they can be ever vigilant. Watching and waiting for an earthquake, tsunami or other catastrophy. All systems where time is of the essence rely on machines and computers to identify a problem and either inform or rectify the situation. For instance in an airplane. An airplane will tell you when your altitude is too low, in some cases pulling you up. Meteorology heavily relies on using programs to predict the weather and to keep track of atmospheric movements.
